# AI Assistance Statement for Early Brainstorming

This document explains the AI-assisted brainstorming shown in the attached screenshots.  
The screenshots correspond to the **early ideation stage** of the DSAI5207 course project and are included to disclose limited AI use transparently.

## 1. Purpose of the AI interaction

At the beginning of the project, AI was used only to support **topic exploration and dataset screening**.  
The goal was to narrow down a feasible project direction that would be:

- manageable within the course timeline,
- suitable for image classification experiments,
- easy to explain in the final presentation,
- and stable enough to produce interpretable results.

## 2. What the screenshots show

### Screenshot 1: Candidate project directions
The AI suggested several possible project ideas for a deep learning course project, including:

1. CNN vs MLP on small image datasets  
2. Deeper MLP not always better  
3. GRU vs vanilla RNN on long/short sequences  
4. Pooling strategy in CNN  
5. Simple gating mechanism for MLP  

From this brainstorming step, the team identified **CNN vs MLP on small image datasets** as the most feasible direction.

### Screenshot 2: Dataset recommendation
After the project direction was narrowed down, AI was further asked to suggest datasets suitable for a **CNN vs MLP low-data project**.  
The AI recommended several candidate datasets, including:

- Rock-Paper-Scissors  
- Garbage Classification  
- Intel Image Classification  

The response also explained why **Rock-Paper-Scissors** would be the most suitable option for a manageable course project, especially in terms of:

- small and clear dataset size,

- simple 3-class classification setting,

- low implementation risk,

- and suitability for repeated experiments under different data ratios.

  ![image-20260422165812083](C:\Users\Lenovo\AppData\Roaming\Typora\typora-user-images\image-20260422165812083.png)

### Screenshot 3: Dataset filtering criteria
The AI also provided general advice on what kinds of datasets to avoid, such as:

- datasets with too many classes,
- datasets without clear official splits,
- highly imbalanced datasets,
- and datasets with very inconsistent image sizes or backgrounds.

This was used only as an initial screening reference when selecting the dataset.

## 3. Scope of AI assistance

The AI assistance in these screenshots was limited to:

- brainstorming possible project topics,
- comparing candidate datasets,
- and discussing practical experiment feasibility.

The AI was **not used to complete the core project work**.
